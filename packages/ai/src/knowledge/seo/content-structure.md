# SEO Content Structure

## Hub-Spoke Architecture

The hub-spoke model organizes content around topic clusters for maximum search authority.

**Hub (pillar page):** Targets the broad keyword. Comprehensive coverage (2500-4000 words). Links out to all spoke pages in the cluster.

**Spokes (supporting pages):** Target long-tail keywords within the same topic. More focused depth (1200-2000 words). Each links back to the hub and to 1-2 related spokes.

**How it works:** Google interprets the internal linking pattern as topical authority. A hub page with 5-7 well-linked spokes signals deeper coverage than a standalone article, even if the standalone article is longer.

**Example cluster:**
- Hub: "AI Marketing Automation" (broad, definitional)
- Spoke: "Best AI Marketing Tools" (commercial, comparison)
- Spoke: "How to Build AI Marketing Workflows" (how-to)
- Spoke: "AI vs Traditional Marketing Automation" (comparison)
- Spoke: "AI Marketing Examples and Case Studies" (proof, validation)

---

## Content Hierarchy

**H1:** One per page. Matches the primary keyword. This is the page title.

**H2:** Main sections. Should be scannable as a standalone table of contents. Use searchable questions or keyword variations. PAA (People Also Ask) questions make strong H2s.

**H3:** Subsections within an H2. Break up long sections. Not every H2 needs H3s.

**Rules:**
- Never skip levels (H1 to H3 with no H2)
- Headers are for structure, not decoration
- Each H2 should make sense if someone reads only the headers
- Front-load keywords in headers where natural

---

## Internal Linking

3-5 contextual links per article. Links should appear within the body text at natural reference points, not in a "related articles" block at the bottom.

**Anchor text:** Use descriptive text that matches the target page's keyword. "Learn more about [keyword-rich description]" -- never "click here."

**Link direction:**
- Spokes link to their hub (passes authority upward)
- Hub links to all its spokes (distributes authority)
- Related spokes cross-link where contextually relevant
- New content links to existing high-performing pages
- Existing high-performing pages should be updated to link to new content

**Avoid:** Orphan pages (no internal links pointing to them), excessive links that dilute focus, linking to low-quality or unrelated pages.

## Content Length by Type

| Content Type | Target Length | Purpose |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Hub/Pillar page | 2500-4000 words | Comprehensive topic coverage |
| Spoke/Supporting article | 1200-2000 words | Focused long-tail keyword targeting |
| How-to tutorial | 2000-3000 words | Step-by-step implementation |
| Comparison page | 2500-4000 words | Commercial intent, detailed evaluation |
| Product/landing page | 800-1500 words | Transactional intent, conversion-focused |
| FAQ/glossary entry | 300-800 words | Quick-answer informational intent |

Length should be determined by what the topic requires and what the SERP rewards -- not by arbitrary word count targets. If top-ranking competitors are 1500 words, a 4000-word article is not inherently better. Match depth to intent.
