# Keyword Intent

## 4 Intent Types

Every search query has an underlying intent. Matching content type to intent is the single most important SEO decision after keyword selection.

**Informational:** The searcher wants to learn or understand something.
Signals: "how to," "what is," "why does," "guide," "tutorial," "examples"
Example: "how to automate email marketing"

**Navigational:** The searcher wants to reach a specific brand or page.
Signals: brand name, product name, "[brand] login," "[brand] pricing"
Example: "Mailchimp login"

**Commercial:** The searcher is evaluating options before a purchase decision.
Signals: "best," "vs," "review," "comparison," "alternative to," "top [category] for [use case]"
Example: "best email marketing tools for small business"

**Transactional:** The searcher is ready to buy or take a specific action.
Signals: "buy," "pricing," "discount," "free trial," "sign up," "coupon"
Example: "Mailchimp pricing plans"

## SaaS Keyword Categories

**Product-led (feature keywords):** Target people searching for capabilities your product provides.
- "[Feature] software" or "[Feature] tool"
- "automated [workflow]" or "[task] automation"
- These attract solution-aware prospects who know what they need

**Content-led (problem keywords):** Target people searching for solutions to problems your product solves.
- "how to [solve problem]" or "why [problem happens]"
- "[Problem] for [audience]"
- These attract problem-aware prospects who need education before conversion

**Competitor keywords:** Target people evaluating alternatives.
- "[Competitor] vs [your brand]"
- "[Competitor] alternative"
- "[Competitor] review"
- High commercial intent -- these searchers are close to a decision

**Integration/partner keywords:** Target people using tools that connect to yours.
- "[Partner tool] + [your category]"
- "how to connect [tool A] and [tool B]"
- Lower volume but very high intent and specificity

## Intent to Content Type Mapping

| Intent | Content Type | Conversion Path |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Informational | Blog post, guide, tutorial, explainer | Email capture, newsletter signup |
| Commercial | Comparison page, listicle, review, "best X" | Free trial CTA, demo request |
| Transactional | Product page, pricing page, landing page | Direct purchase, signup |
| Navigational | Homepage, product pages, docs | Existing user retention |

**Mismatching intent kills rankings.** A blog post will not rank for "pricing" queries. A pricing page will not rank for "how to" queries. Check what content types appear on page 1 of the SERP for any keyword -- that is what Google has determined matches the intent.

## Keyword Clustering

Group related keywords into clusters. One page per cluster. This prevents cannibalization (multiple pages competing for the same keyword).

**Clustering process:**
1. Group by semantic similarity -- keywords that mean similar things
2. Group by search intent -- keywords with the same user goal
3. Identify the pillar keyword (broadest term) for each cluster
4. Assign supporting keywords (more specific variations) to the same page

**Cannibalization check:** If two pages on the same site could reasonably rank for the same keyword, they are competing against each other. Consolidate into one stronger page or differentiate their intent clearly.

**Cluster validation:** Search the pillar keyword. If the top results are the same pages that rank for the supporting keywords, they belong in one cluster. If different pages rank, they may be separate clusters.

**One page, one cluster.** Each page targets one primary keyword and naturally incorporates 3-8 supporting keywords from the same cluster. Do not force unrelated keywords onto a single page.
