# CTA Patterns

## CTA Psychology

Action verbs create momentum. Benefit framing answers "what do I get?" not "what do I do?"

Urgency must be real -- manufactured urgency (fake countdown timers, "only 3 left" on digital products) erodes trust. Legitimate urgency: limited-time pricing, cohort-based enrollment, seasonal relevance.

The CTA is not where you convince -- it is where you make it easy for someone already convinced. If the CTA is not converting, the problem is usually upstream in the copy.

---

## Button Copy Patterns

**Benefit-framed (strong):**
- "Get the report" > "Download"
- "Start free trial" > "Sign up"
- "See the exact template" > "Learn more"
- "Start building" > "Buy now"
- "Send me the first lesson free" > "Subscribe"

**Weak defaults to avoid:**
- "Submit" -- feels like homework
- "Learn more" -- no specificity
- "Click here" -- meaningless
- "Sign up" -- describes the action, not the benefit

**The test:** Does the button copy complete the sentence "I want to ___"?
- "I want to... get the report" works
- "I want to... submit" does not

## Risk Reversal

Remove the fear of making a wrong decision.

**Money-back guarantee:** "30-day money-back, no questions asked." State the timeframe and the absence of friction.

**Free trial:** Let the product prove itself before asking for payment. "No credit card required" removes the last objection.

**Social proof as risk reversal:** "Join 2,600+ marketers" implies that many people have already validated this decision.

**Speed/ease as risk reversal:** "2 minutes to install" or "Set up in one afternoon" reduces perceived commitment.

**Below-CTA formula:** [Risk reversal] + [Social proof] + [Speed/ease]
Example: "$199 once. Join 2,600+ marketers. 2 minutes to install."

---

## CTA Placement

**Above the fold:** For most-aware audiences who already know what they want. Pair with a direct headline.

**After proof:** Place a CTA immediately after testimonials, case studies, or data. The reader is at peak belief.

**At natural decision points:** After you have answered the reader's main objection or completed a compelling argument. Do not interrupt the slippery slide -- place CTAs where the reader would naturally pause.

**End of page:** The final CTA should include friction reducers (guarantee, social proof, ease). This is the last chance for readers who consumed the entire page.

**Repetition:** On long-form pages, repeat the CTA 2-3 times at natural breaks. Same offer, same button copy. Do not introduce new offers mid-page.
