# Headline Patterns

Headlines do 80% of the work. One headline can outpull another by 19.5x -- same product, same offer, different headline.

## 5 Headline Pattern Types

**How-To:** Bulletproof and direct. "How to [outcome]" with optional modifiers.
- "How to [outcome] without [common pain]"
- "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]"
- "How to [outcome] even if [objection]"

**List/Number:** Numbers stop the scroll. Odd numbers outperform even. Specific beats round (37 beats 40).
- "[Number] [Ways/Mistakes/Secrets] to [Outcome]"
- Lower numbers for essentials ("3 things that matter"), higher for comprehensive guides ("101 tips")

**Question:** Self-selects the right reader. Must match a question they already ask themselves.
- "Do you [common mistake/struggle]?"
- "What if you could [desirable outcome]?"
- Ran 40+ years: "Do You Make These Mistakes in English?"

**Command/Direct:** Works when audience is highly aware and just needs the offer.
- "Stop [common but ineffective action]"
- "Get [specific benefit]"
- "Write Faster. Convert More."

**Story-Lead:** Complete narrative arc in one line. Creates curiosity through tension.
- "They [doubted] when I [action]... But when I [result]..."
- "From [bad state] to [good state]"
- Caples' piano ad: story in 15 words with universal emotion (embarrassment to triumph)

## Headline Formulas

**Master formula:** [Action verb] + [Specific outcome] + [Timeframe or contrast]
- "Ship your startup in days, not weeks"
- "Save 4 hours per person every single week"

**Specificity formula:** [Specific number/metric] + [Unexpected comparison or detail]
- "I tested 47 headlines. One pattern beat everything else by 3x."
- "After 10 years and $45M in sales, I've found exactly 3 things that matter."

**Reason-why formula:** "Why [desirable outcome happens]" or "[Number] Reasons Why [claim]"
- "Why Your Ads Aren't Working (And What to Do Instead)"

**Warning formula:** "Don't [action] until [condition]" or "[Number] Mistakes [audience] Make"
- "Warning: Your Landing Page Is Losing 73% of Visitors"

**Transformation formula:** "From [before] to [after]" or "How I Went from [before] to [after]"
- "From Zero to $10K MRR in 6 Months"

**Contrarian formula:** "[Common belief] Is Wrong. Here's Why." or "Stop [common advice]"
- Must deliver on the promise. Contrarian for clickbait's sake backfires.

## Awareness-Level Headlines

| Awareness Level | Best Patterns | Example |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| Unaware | Story-lead, question, provocative claim | "The diary of a bored housewife" |
| Problem-Aware | Question, how-to (problem-focused) | "Are you ever tongue-tied at a party?" |
| Solution-Aware | How-to (mechanism), specificity, reason-why | "A new way to remove wrinkles without surgery" |
| Product-Aware | Number/proof, command, warning | "Join 10,000 happy customers" |
| Most Aware | Direct/command, offer-led | "$199 today only" |

## Testing Methodology

- Write at least 5 headline variations. Ogilvy wrote 26 for Rolls-Royce.
- Self-interest beats cleverness every time.
- News hooks attention: "New," "Announcing," "Finally," "Just released."
- Curiosity alone fails. Curiosity + benefit works. Pure curiosity = clickbait.
- Specifics beat generalities: "$327" beats "money."
- Longer headlines can outperform short ones if every word earns its place.
- A/B test one variable at a time: the hook, the number, the framing, the promise.
- Measure by the action that matters (clicks, conversions), not opinions.
