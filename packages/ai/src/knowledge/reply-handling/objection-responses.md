# Objection Response Framework

Proof escalation ladder for handling objections in outbound replies. Every objection is a buying signal - it means they're thinking about it.

---

## Core Principle: Proof Escalation

Never argue. Escalate the proof. Each objection type has a ladder of evidence, starting with the lightest touch and escalating only if needed.

## Common Objections

### "Too expensive" / Price Objection

**Level 1 - Reframe value:**
"Totally fair question. Most of our customers found that [product] saves them [X hours/week], which works out to [dollar value]. The question isn't really cost - it's ROI."

**Level 2 - Social proof:**
"[Similar company] had the same concern. After 3 months they calculated [specific ROI metric]. Happy to share the breakdown."

**Level 3 - Risk reversal:**
"What if we started with [smaller scope/trial] so you could measure the impact before committing to the full solution?"

### "We already use [competitor]"

**Level 1 - Acknowledge + differentiate:**
"[Competitor] is solid for [what they're good at]. Where we differ is [specific differentiator relevant to their situation]. Most teams use both during transition."

**Level 2 - Competitive displacement:**
"We've helped [N companies] switch from [competitor]. The main reasons were [specific gaps]. Does any of that resonate?"

**Level 3 - Side-by-side:**
"Would it be useful to see a side-by-side of how [competitor] handles [their pain point] vs. our approach? No pitch - just the comparison."

### "Not the right time"

**Level 1 - Anchor to signal:**
"Understood. I reached out because [specific signal - hiring, funding, etc.] usually means [problem] becomes urgent. When would be better?"

**Level 2 - Value-add bridge:**
"No problem. In the meantime, [resource/guide/benchmark] might be useful for when you're ready. Mind if I send it over?"

**Level 3 - Specific follow-up:**
"Got it. Can I check back in [specific timeframe]? I'll have [new case study/feature/data] that might be relevant then."

### "Need to check with my team"

**Level 1 - Enable the champion:**
"Of course. Would it help if I put together a one-pager your team can review? I'll include [specific data points they'll care about]."

**Level 2 - Offer to present:**
"Happy to do a 15-minute overview for the team. That way I can answer their specific questions directly."

**Level 3 - Multi-thread:**
"Who on your team would have the most questions? I can reach out to them separately so they have context before your internal discussion."

### "Send me more information"

This is often a soft rejection disguised as interest. Test it.

**Level 1 - Qualify the request:**
"Absolutely. What specifically would be most useful? [Feature details / pricing / case studies / integrations]" - if they specify, they're real. If they go silent, it was a brush-off.

**Level 2 - Conditional send:**
"I'll send that over. Would it make sense to schedule 15 minutes next week to walk through it together? That way I can tailor it to your situation."

## Response Rules

1. **Never send more than one response to an objection in 24 hours.** Give them space to process.
2. **Mirror their energy level.** Short reply = short response. Detailed reply = detailed response.
3. **Always end with a question, not a statement.** Keep the conversation alive.
4. **If they object twice on the same point, accept it.** Persistence becomes pressure after two rounds.
5. **Never disparage the competitor directly.** "They're solid for X, we're different in Y."
6. **Reference specific data, not generic claims.** "[Company] saved 10 hours/week" not "our customers love us."
