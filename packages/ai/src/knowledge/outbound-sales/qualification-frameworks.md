# Qualification Frameworks

Structured qualification for AI-assisted outbound. Adapted from BANT, MEDDIC, and SPIN for signal-driven selling.

---

## Signal-First Qualification

Traditional frameworks ask the prospect directly. Signal-first qualification gathers most answers from data before the conversation.

**Pre-call qualification (from enrichment + signals):**
- Budget: funding stage, company size, existing tool spend (BuiltWith)
- Authority: org chart mapping, title seniority, LinkedIn role
- Need: signal type (hiring = active need, content engagement = researching)
- Timeline: signal recency (fresh signal = likely urgent)

**On-call qualification (confirm and deepen):**
- Validate pre-call hypotheses
- Uncover the "why now" behind the signal
- Map the decision process (who else, what steps, what timeline)
- Identify blockers (budget cycle, competing priorities, incumbent contract)

## BANT for Outbound

Adapted for cold outreach context where you earn the right to qualify.

**Budget:** Don't ask "what's your budget?" Instead:
- "How are you handling [problem] today?"
- "What's that costing you in [time/money/opportunity]?"
- Infer from company size, funding, existing tools

**Authority:** Don't ask "are you the decision-maker?" Instead:
- "Who else would need to weigh in on something like this?"
- "How have you evaluated tools like this before?"
- Map from org chart: VP+ for enterprise, founder for SMB

**Need:** Don't pitch. Confirm:
- "I noticed [signal]. Is [problem] something you're actively working on?"
- "What would solving this look like for your team?"
- Score: active search > acknowledged problem > unaware

**Timeline:** Don't ask "when do you need this?" Instead:
- "Is there a deadline or event driving this?"
- "What happens if this doesn't get solved this quarter?"
- Infer from signal urgency: job posting closing date, funding deployment timeline

## MEDDIC for Enterprise Deals

When deal size justifies deeper qualification.

**Metrics:** What measurable outcome matters?
- "What would success look like in numbers?"
- "How are you measuring [problem area] today?"

**Economic Buyer:** Who signs the check?
- Usually not your first contact
- "Who owns the budget for tools in this area?"

**Decision Criteria:** What are they evaluating on?
- "What matters most when you evaluate solutions?"
- Map to your differentiators

**Decision Process:** How do they buy?
- "Walk me through how your team has evaluated tools before"
- Legal, procurement, security review requirements

**Identify Pain:** Make it specific and quantified
- "How many hours per week does your team spend on [task]?"
- "What's the impact when [problem] happens?"

**Champion:** Who will fight for you internally?
- Your primary contact who experiences the pain daily
- Arm them with internal selling materials

## Disqualification Signals

Save time by recognizing early exits:
- No budget and no funding expected
- Pain is "nice to have" not urgent
- Incumbent contract with 12+ months remaining and no dissatisfaction
- Decision-maker unreachable after 3 attempts through champion
- Company size below minimum viable (cost to serve exceeds LTV)
- Regulatory or compliance barrier to adoption
