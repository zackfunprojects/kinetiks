# Personalization Depth

Not all outreach requires the same level of personalization. Over-personalizing wastes effort. Under-personalizing wastes the recipient's attention.

---

## 4 Levels of Personalization

### Level 1: Name + Company
Minimum viable personalization. "Hi Sarah, I noticed [Company] is..." -- this is table stakes. Almost everyone does this. It barely registers as personalization anymore.

**Use when:** High-volume campaigns, retargeting ads, broad nurture sequences.

### Level 2: + Role + Industry Vertical
Adds role-specific framing and industry context. "As a VP of Marketing in fintech, you are probably dealing with..." -- this shows you know their category.

**Use when:** LinkedIn outreach, segmented email campaigns, industry-specific landing pages.

### Level 3: + Specific Trigger Event
References something that actually happened to them or their company recently. "I saw [Company] just opened a London office..." -- this proves you did research and have a reason for reaching out now.

**Use when:** Cold email (this is the minimum for cold outreach to get replies), targeted account-based campaigns, executive outreach.

### Level 4: + Mutual Connection, Shared Context, Referenced Content
References their specific writing, a shared connection, an event you both attended, or a precise detail from their public activity. "Your point about attribution modeling in last week's post resonated..." -- this is a genuine conversation starter.

**Use when:** High-value target accounts, C-suite outreach, strategic partnership introductions.

---

## Personalization-to-Channel Fit

| Channel | Minimum Level | Why |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Retargeting ads | Level 1 | They already visited -- recognition is enough |
| Blog / SEO content | Level 0 (broad) | Audience is unknown at read time |
| Segmented email nurture | Level 2 | Role + industry makes it feel relevant |
| LinkedIn connection request | Level 2 | Role context justifies the connection |
| Cold email | Level 3 | Without a trigger event, it reads as spam |
| Executive cold outreach | Level 4 | Executives delete anything that feels mass-sent |
| ABM direct mail | Level 3+ | Physical mail needs a clear reason for contact |

**The cost rule:** Each personalization level roughly doubles the research time. Only go deeper when the target value justifies it.

## Trigger Event Library

Trigger events provide the "why now" for outreach. Each type signals something specific about the company's situation.

### Hiring Signals
- **New role posted:** Budget has been allocated for that function. If they are hiring a Head of Data, they are investing in data infrastructure.
- **Leadership change:** New executives rewrite strategy in their first 90 days. They are open to new vendors.
- **Rapid hiring:** Growth mode. Systems that worked for 50 people break at 200.

### Funding Signals
- **Raised a round:** Spending mode activated. They have a board expecting them to deploy capital and hit growth targets.
- **IPO preparation:** Compliance, reporting, and scalability become urgent overnight.
- **Acquisition:** Integration challenges create new tool needs.

### Technology Signals
- **Adopted a new tool:** Integration opportunity. If they just bought a CRM, they need everything that connects to it.
- **Deprecated a tool:** Replacement window is open. They are actively evaluating alternatives.
- **Job postings mention specific tech:** Reveals their stack and where they are investing.

### Content Signals
- **Published an article or talk:** They are a thought leader on that topic. Engage on their ideas, not your pitch.
- **Commented on industry trends:** Reveals their perspective. Align your angle with their stated worldview.
- **Launched a podcast or newsletter:** Content-forward mindset. They value expertise and insight.

### Pain Signals
- **Negative review of a competitor:** Actively dissatisfied. They are looking.
- **Public complaint about a process:** Named their own pain. Mirror it back.
- **Regulatory change in their industry:** New compliance requirements create urgent tool needs.

## Signal-to-Message Mapping

Each trigger type maps to a specific opening approach:

| Trigger Type | Opening Angle | Example Opening |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Hiring signal | "Investing in [function] suggests..." | "Saw you are building out the data team -- companies at that stage usually hit [specific problem]." |
| Funding signal | "With the new round, [growth challenge]..." | "Post-Series B is when [specific scaling issue] usually surfaces." |
| Tech signal | "Now that you are on [tool], [integration angle]..." | "Teams running [tool] typically need [specific integration] within 90 days." |
| Content signal | "Your take on [topic] was [specific reaction]..." | "Your point about [specific claim] in [piece] -- we see the same pattern in our data." |
| Pain signal | "The [specific problem] you mentioned..." | "Noticed the [specific frustration]. That usually comes from [root cause]." |

**Never fake a trigger.** If you cannot find a genuine trigger event, drop to Level 2 personalization and use role + industry framing instead. A fabricated trigger is worse than no trigger -- it destroys trust instantly.
