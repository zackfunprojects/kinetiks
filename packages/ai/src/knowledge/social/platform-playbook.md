# Platform Playbook

Per-platform rules for content creation and distribution. Each platform has distinct algorithm signals, formats, and audience expectations.

---

## LinkedIn

**Algorithm signals:** Dwell time is #1. Topic authority from consistent niche posting. Native content (no external links) gets significantly more reach. First 60 minutes determine whether post reaches 2nd/3rd-degree connections.

**Post format:**
- Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences (mobile readability)
- Long-form text posts: 1000-1300 characters outperform short
- First line must hook -- no "Excited to announce..." or "Great news!"
- Put links in comments, never in post body (kills reach)
- 3-5 hashtags maximum, at end of post
- Post 3-5x/week

**Carousels:**
- 8-10 slides, one idea per slide
- 1080x1350px (4:5 ratio) for maximum feed space
- Slide 1: bold hook with curiosity. Slides 2-7: one point each. Slide 8: summary. Slide 9: CTA (save, follow, repost)
- Font minimum 24pt body, 40pt+ headlines
- Educational content performs best; save-worthy = algorithm gold

**Common mistakes:** Posting links in main body. Wall of text with no line breaks. Too many hashtags. Copying Twitter's casual energy.

## Twitter/X

**Algorithm signals:** Replies (highest weight), quote tweets (2x value vs retweet), time spent on tweet, media boost, profile clicks. First tweet determines 90% of thread success.

**Single tweets:** 280 chars. Bold claim or sharp insight. Stand alone as complete thoughts.

**Threads:**
- Hook tweet must work as a standalone post
- Structure: hook > setup/context > content points (numbered) > summary (TL;DR) > CTA
- 3-5 tweets for feature announcements, 8-15 for deep dives
- Add value in every tweet -- no filler
- Visual in tweet 3 to sustain engagement
- Media per tweet boosts engagement

**Hook formulas:** Bold claim + proof. Specific number + insight. Observation > lesson. Contrarian take.

**Common mistakes:** Threads over 20 tweets. No hook in first tweet. Filler tweets. Not replying to comments.

## Instagram

**Algorithm signals:** DM shares ("sends per reach") is strongest discovery signal. Saves critical for Feed/Explore. Watch time and completion rate for Reels. Early velocity in first 30-90 minutes.

**Carousels:**
- Highest engagement format, best for saves and depth
- 5-10 slides (up to 20 in some regions)
- Educational, text-heavy content works
- Slide 1: stop-scroll hook. Slides 2-7: value. Slide 8-9: recap. Slide 10: CTA
- Swipe-through rate signals interest to algorithm

**Reels:**
- Hook in first 2-3 seconds or lose 65% of viewers
- Start mid-action, never with intro
- 7-15 seconds: highest completion rate
- 30-60 seconds: acceptable if value is dense
- Captions required (80%+ watch without sound)
- Trending audio boosts discovery

**Captions:** Up to 2200 characters. Three formats that work: story (hook > personal story > lesson > question), list (number + benefit > points > engagement question), teach (contrarian statement > explanation > action steps).

**Photos:** Getting renewed algorithm support. Still viable for photo-native audiences.

**Common mistakes:** Static images only. No captions on video. Reposting TikToks with watermark. Inconsistent visual style.

---

## TikTok

**Algorithm signals:** Watch time (first seconds weighted heavily), completion rate, rewatch rate, shares (especially DMs), niche community alignment. Videos tested in batches of 200-500 people; each batch must perform for the next to see it.

**Format rules:**
- Hook in first 2-3 seconds or die. This is non-negotiable.
- 15-60 seconds (algorithm now favors 30-60+ if retention is high)
- Raw/authentic > polished production
- Trending sounds boost reach (even at low volume)
- Captions required (80%+ watch without sound)
- Loop potential (end where you started) drives rewatches

**Hook categories:** Identity callout ("This is for my [group]..."), pattern interrupt (start mid-action), controversy spark ("I'm gonna get hate for this..."), proof first (show result then explain), curiosity gap.

**Video structures:**
- Rapid teach (15-30s): hook > 3-5 rapid points > CTA
- Story hook (30-60s): start in the middle > context > tension > payoff
- Myth bust (15-45s): "Stop believing [myth]" > truth > action
- POV (15-30s): relatable scenario > act out > punchline

**Common mistakes:** Slow intros. No captions. Ignoring trending sounds. Over-produced content. Not responding to comments.

## YouTube

**Algorithm signals:** Click-through rate (CTR), average view duration + % watched, session impact (keeps people on platform), viewer satisfaction (surveys + behavior). Topical consistency builds channel authority.

**Shorts:** Discovery engine. 10-35 seconds optimal. Drives subscribers. Can repurpose TikTok content. Patterns: rapid tip, best clip from long-form, transformation, reaction.

**Long-form:** 8-12 minutes sweet spot. Search + browse for evergreen discovery. Structure (AIDA): Attention (0-30s, pattern interrupt + preview) > Interest (30s-2min, why it matters) > Desire (main content, clear sections with examples) > Action (last 30s, summary + subscribe + next video).

**Thumbnails:** Maximum 3 visual elements. Expressive face + bold text (3-4 words) + contrast colors. CTR benchmarks: 4-10% typical, 6-12% strong.

**Titles:** "How I [result] in [timeframe]", "[Number] [things] that [outcome]", "Why [surprising thing] is [counterintuitive truth]".

**Common mistakes:** Clickbait without delivery. No hook in first 30 seconds. Cluttered thumbnails. No end screens/cards.

## Threads

**Algorithm signals:** Reply depth, reshares, quote-posts with commentary, cross-graph engagement, Instagram relationship signals carry over.

**Format:** 500 character limit. Text-forward, opinion-led content. Conversational tone wins. No hashtag strategy -- content signals matter. Reply culture is the point.

**What works:** Conversational takes with genuine questions. Mini-threads (multi-post). Quote-post commentary adding your take. Cross-reference Instagram content for deeper discussion.

**Current advantage:** Lower competition than Twitter/X. High organic reach (no ads yet). More civil reply culture.

---

## Bluesky

**Algorithm signals:** Likes, reposts, reply chains, custom feed subscriptions. Chronological timeline still default for many users. Decentralized (AT Protocol).

**Format:** 300 character limit. Thoughtful, substantive writing outperforms hot takes. Nuanced takes, experience reports, useful curation.

**Audience:** Tech-savvy, media-literate. Journalists, academics, developers. Quality over volume. Use domain-as-handle for credibility.

**Custom feeds:** The killer feature. Topic-specific algorithmic feeds anyone can create or subscribe to. Target niche audiences with precision.

---

## Reddit

**Algorithm signals:** Upvote/downvote ratio, velocity of upvotes in first hour, comment count and depth, subreddit-specific norms, account age/karma.

**Rules:** Value-first or get removed. 90/10 rule: 90% value, 10% self-reference. Disclose affiliations or get banned. Long-form, detailed posts outperform short ones. Pseudonymous -- content quality matters more than personal brand.

**What works:** Detailed breakdowns with data. Honest experience reports. AMAs (Ask Me Anything). Comment strategy (add genuine value to trending posts in niche subreddits).

**SEO opportunity:** Google surfaces Reddit content in search results. High-trust environment. Longer shelf life than social posts.

---

## Blog/Website

**SEO-first approach:**
- 1500-3000 words for ranking potential
- Internal linking between related content
- Schema markup for rich snippets
- Target one primary keyword + 3-5 related terms per post
- Headers (H2/H3) should include keyword variants
- Meta descriptions: 150-160 chars, include primary keyword, drive clicks

---

## Cross-Platform Cadence

**Weekly rhythm (5-3-2):**
- 5 posts/week: value content (carousels, threads, educational)
- 3 posts/week: engagement content (questions, polls, hot takes)
- 2 posts/week: personal/behind-the-scenes

**Platform-specific CTAs:** LinkedIn (repost, save). Twitter (RT first tweet, bookmark thread). Instagram (save, send to someone). TikTok (follow, "Part 2?", save). YouTube (subscribe + bell, watch next). Threads (reply, repost). Bluesky (reply, repost, add to feed). Reddit (happy to answer questions, offer Part 2).
