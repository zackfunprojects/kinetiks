# Content Atomization

One long-form piece becomes 8+ platform-native variants. This is not copy-paste at different lengths -- each variant is rebuilt from scratch for the platform's native format, audience expectations, and algorithm signals.

---

## The Atomization Process

1. Create one pillar piece (blog post, podcast episode, video, newsletter, whitepaper)
2. Extract the core insights, data points, stories, and frameworks
3. Rebuild each insight as a platform-native piece -- different hook, different structure, different tone
4. Each variant should feel like it was created for that platform, not adapted from somewhere else

**Key principle:** Atomization is deconstruction and reconstruction, not compression. A LinkedIn post derived from a blog is not a shorter blog -- it is a LinkedIn post that happens to share a source insight.

---

## Source to Variants Map

**Blog post produces:**
- LinkedIn post: extract the single most compelling insight, frame as personal observation or professional lesson
- Twitter/X thread: restructure as step-by-step breakdown or numbered lessons
- Instagram carousel: visual breakdown of the framework or process, one point per slide
- Newsletter snippet: personal take on the same topic, more intimate voice
- TikTok script: find the most controversial or surprising angle, 30-60 second treatment
- Facebook post: story version with community-building question at end
- YouTube Short: the single most visual or demonstrable point
- Reddit post: detailed breakdown with data, positioned as value-first community contribution

**Podcast/video produces:**
- Quote posts (LinkedIn, Twitter): the sharpest 1-2 sentence moments
- Audiogram or clip (Instagram Reel, TikTok): the most engaging 30-60 second segment
- Thread (Twitter): key takeaways structured as lessons
- Blog post: expanded transcript of the best segment with added context
- Carousel (LinkedIn, Instagram): framework or process discussed, visualized

---

## Adaptation Rules

**Change the hook per platform.** The same insight needs a different entry point on LinkedIn (professional credibility) vs TikTok (pattern interrupt) vs Twitter (bold claim) vs Instagram (visual arrest).

**Adjust length to platform norms.** LinkedIn: 1000-1300 chars. Twitter: 280 chars or 5-15 tweet thread. Instagram caption: up to 2200 chars. TikTok: 15-60 second script. Blog: 1500-3000 words.

**Adjust tone.** LinkedIn: more professional, thought-leadership framing. Twitter: more casual, punchy, opinionated. Instagram: visual-first, educational or aspirational. TikTok: raw, direct, personality-forward. Reddit: detailed, transparent, community-native.

**Add platform-specific CTAs.** LinkedIn: "Repost to help someone in your network." Twitter: "RT the first tweet." Instagram: "Save this for later." TikTok: "Follow for Part 2." Each CTA matches how the platform's algorithm rewards engagement.

**Respect native formats.** Do not post a Twitter thread on LinkedIn. Do not post a LinkedIn essay on TikTok. The format IS the message on social platforms.

---

## Frequency and Cadence

- One pillar piece per week (blog, podcast, or video)
- Produces 5-8 social posts across platforms
- Plus 1 newsletter edition
- Equals sustained multi-platform presence from a single content investment

**Spacing:** Do not publish all variants on the same day. Spread across the week. The blog publishes Monday, LinkedIn carousel Tuesday, Twitter thread Wednesday, Instagram Reel Thursday, newsletter Friday.

**Evergreen recycling:** High-performing atomized content can be refreshed and reposted 60-90 days later with updated hooks. Most followers never saw the original.
