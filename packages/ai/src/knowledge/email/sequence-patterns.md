# Email Sequence Patterns

Reference for building email sequences that convert subscribers into customers. Covers seven core archetypes with timing, purpose, and CTA guidance for each email.

---

## Welcome Sequence (6-7 emails)

The most important sequence. First impressions compound. Framework: DELIVER > CONNECT > VALUE > BRIDGE > PITCH.

| Email | Day | Purpose | CTA |
|-------|-----|---------|-----|
| 1: Delivery | 0 | Deliver the lead magnet, set expectations, get first micro-engagement | Download + reply with a question |
| 2: Origin Story | 1-2 | Build rapport through vulnerability and shared experience | Read / engage (no sell) |
| 3: Quick Win | 3-4 | Teach something useful, demonstrate expertise, create a quick win | Take one specific action |
| 4: Deep Value | 5-6 | Continue building authority with a different angle or topic | Apply the insight |
| 5: Bridge | 7-8 | Show the gap between where they are and where they could be; introduce paid offer concept without pitching | Curiosity — hint at what is coming |
| 6: Soft Pitch | 10 | Introduce the offer properly, handle objections, let them self-select | Visit sales page or learn more |
| 7: Direct Pitch | 12-14 | Make the clear ask, create urgency if authentic | Buy / sign up |

**Key principles:**
- Email 1 sends immediately. Include a quick-start action they can complete in under 5 minutes.
- Email 2 uses a specific story (moment or realization), not a corporate bio.
- Emails 3-4 are pure value. 80% of the sequence is trust-building.
- Email 5 is the pivot: acknowledge what the free resource does, reveal what it cannot do, paint what is possible with the full solution.
- Email 6 includes "who it's for" and "who it's NOT for" — disqualification builds trust.
- Email 7 is direct. No long buildup. Restate core value in one sentence, handle the biggest remaining objection, add real urgency if it exists.

## Nurture Sequence (4-6 weeks)

Educational drip that builds trust between welcome and conversion. Ratio: 80% value, 20% soft sell.

**Structure per email:**
1. Hook — insight, observation, or question
2. Teach — one actionable idea
3. Proof — example, case study, or data
4. Soft bridge — brief mention of how the product relates (not a pitch)

**Timing:** Weekly or 2x/week. Consistency matters more than optimal day.

**Content mix across the sequence:**
- 2-3 tactical how-to emails (specific, implementable)
- 1 myth-busting or contrarian take
- 1 case study or customer story
- 1 curated resources or tools roundup
- Occasional personal story to maintain human connection

**When to transition to conversion:** After 4-6 nurture emails, or when engagement signals are strong (high open rates, replies, link clicks).

## Conversion Sequence (5-7 emails)

Urgency-escalation sequence designed to close. Framework: OPEN > DESIRE > PROOF > OBJECTION > URGENCY > CLOSE.

| Email | Purpose | Approach |
|-------|---------|----------|
| 1: Open | Introduce the offer, state core promise | Lead with the transformation, not features |
| 2: Desire | Paint the before/after, widen the gap | Vivid future pacing — what life looks like after |
| 3: Proof | Testimonials, case studies, specific results | Let customers make the claim; use specific numbers |
| 4: Objection | Handle the biggest "but..." | Acknowledge the concern honestly, then counter with evidence |
| 5: Urgency | Why now matters | Only use real deadlines — price increase, bonus expiration, limited spots |
| 6: Close | Final push, clear CTA | Direct, short, one ask |
| 7: Last Call | Deadline reminder (if applicable) | Send day-of-deadline; recap what they lose by not acting |

**Timing:** Every 2 days standard. Final 3 emails can compress to 3 consecutive days during a deadline.

**Price-to-trust ratio:**
- Under $100: 3-5 value emails before pitching
- $100-500: 5-7 value emails before pitching
- Over $500: 7-10 value emails or route to sales call

## Launch Sequence (6-10 emails)

Time-bound campaign for product launches, promotions, or cohort opens. Framework: SEED > OPEN > VALUE > PROOF > URGENCY > CLOSE.

**Pre-Launch (Day -3 to -1):**
- 1-2 emails building anticipation
- Seed interest without revealing details
- "Something is coming" creates curiosity

**Cart Open (Day 0-2):**
- Announcement email with full details (morning of Day 0)
- Second angle email (evening of Day 0 or Day 1)
- Value deep-dive: transformation and what is included

**Mid-Launch (Day 3-5):**
- Social proof and testimonials
- Objection handling (FAQ-style or story-driven)
- Case study: one detailed customer result

**Cart Close (Day 6-7):**
- 48-hour warning
- 24-hour warning (morning)
- Final hours (evening)
- Last call (before midnight, if applicable)

**Key principle:** Daily sending is acceptable during a launch because the deadline justifies the intensity. Acknowledge the frequency: "I know I'm emailing more than usual this week."

## Re-engagement Sequence (4 emails)

For subscribers inactive 30+ days. Framework: PATTERN INTERRUPT > VALUE > DECISION > FINAL.

| Email | Purpose | Subject Line Style |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1: Pattern Interrupt | Break the pattern, acknowledge absence | "Did I do something wrong?" / "Still there?" |
| 2: Value Reminder | Deliver your single best piece of content, no ask | Lead with the most useful thing you have |
| 3: Special Offer | Give them a reason to re-engage (exclusive content, discount, early access) | Direct benefit framing |
| 4: Final Notice | Warn you will remove them from the list | "I'm cleaning my list — should I keep you?" |

**Key principles:**
- Email 4 creates real urgency because the consequence (unsubscribe) is genuine.
- If they do not engage after email 4, remove them. A clean list improves deliverability for everyone else.
- Use a different "from" name or subject line style than your regular emails to stand out.

## SaaS Trial-to-Paid Sequence (6 emails)

Guides free trial users from signup to paid conversion. Framework: ONBOARD > ACTIVATE > DISCOVER > CONVERT > EXPAND.

| Email | Day | Purpose | CTA |
|-------|-----|---------|-----|
| 1: Welcome | 0 | Onboarding: one action to get started | Complete setup / first action |
| 2: Activation | 1-2 | Help them reach the first "aha" moment | Use the core feature |
| 3: Milestone | 3-5 | Celebrate progress, introduce next feature | Try second key feature |
| 4: Feature Discovery | 7 | Show a capability they probably haven't found | Explore advanced feature |
| 5: Conversion | 10-12 | Make the case for upgrading before trial ends | Upgrade / choose a plan |
| 6: Expansion | 14+ (post-conversion) | Introduce advanced features or higher tier | Explore premium feature / invite team |

**Key principles:**
- Trigger emails based on behavior, not just time. If they haven't completed onboarding by Day 3, send a help email.
- Track activation milestones (first project created, first integration connected, first result achieved) and tailor messaging.
- The conversion email should focus on what they lose when the trial ends, not just what they gain by upgrading.

## Cross-Product Introduction

How to introduce a sibling or complementary product at the right lifecycle stage.

**Timing rules:**
- Never introduce during onboarding (Days 0-7 of primary product)
- Wait until the customer has achieved a measurable success with the current product
- Ideal window: 30-60 days after purchase, when they are active and satisfied

**Introduction framework:**
1. Celebrate their success with the current product (specific metric or milestone)
2. Name the natural next problem that arises ("Now that you have X, you probably need Y")
3. Introduce the sibling product as the solution to that next problem
4. Keep it low-pressure: "Whenever you're ready" not "Buy now"

**Email structure:**
- Subject: Reference their success, not the new product
- Body: 70% about them and their progress, 30% about the new product
- CTA: Learn more (not buy)

## Sequence Architecture Patterns

**Straight Line:** Email 1 > 2 > 3 > 4 > Pitch. Simple. No branches. Works for short sequences.

**Branch (behavior-based):** After Email 2, split based on engagement. Clicked? Route to pitch sequence. Did not click? Route to more value. Requires automation platform support.

**Hybrid (full lifecycle):** Welcome (5-7 emails) > Wait 7 days > Conversion (5-7 emails) > No purchase? > Nurture (ongoing weekly). Most complete approach.

**One CTA per email.** Multiple CTAs equal no CTAs. Exception: delivery email can include "download" plus "reply with a question."
