# Email Subject Lines

Reference for writing subject lines that get opens without resorting to tricks.

---

## Psychology Patterns

**Curiosity Gap:** Open a loop the reader must click to close.
- "The mistake 90% of founders make"
- "I was wrong about [topic]"
- "Why [surprising thing] actually works"

**Specificity:** Precise numbers and details signal real experience.
- "How I booked $14k in 30 days"
- "$47,329 from one email sequence"
- "3 changes that doubled our reply rate"

**Personalization:** Use merge fields when they add genuine relevance.
- "[First name], quick question"
- "Saw [company name] is hiring — thought of this"
- Works best when combined with a relevant observation, not just name-dropping

**Urgency:** Only use real deadlines. Manufactured urgency erodes trust.
- "Price goes up Friday at midnight"
- "12 hours left"
- "Last chance" — only when it genuinely is

**Direct Benefit:** State what the reader gains.
- "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]"
- "[Number] ways to [benefit]"
- "The fastest way to [result]"

**Question:** Invites mental engagement.
- "Are you making this mistake?"
- "What would you do with [outcome]?"
- "Can I ask you something?"

**Pattern Interrupt:** Breaks inbox monotony.
- "." (just a period)
- "So..."
- "Bad news"
- "[First name]" (name only)

---

## Structural Rules

- **Under 40 characters performs best.** Displays fully on mobile. Every character beyond 40 risks truncation.
- **Preview text is the second headline.** First 40-90 characters appear in inbox preview. Make them complement (not repeat) the subject line. Account for 24% of open-rate decisions.
- **No ALL CAPS.** Triggers spam filters and reads as shouting.
- **Emoji:** Test before committing. Some audiences respond well; others see it as unprofessional. Never use more than one per subject line.
- **Vary your formula.** Do not send three curiosity-gap subjects in a row. Alternate between styles to avoid pattern fatigue.

---

## A/B Testing

- **Test one variable at a time.** Subject line A vs. B, not subject line + send time + preview text simultaneously.
- **Minimum 1,000 recipients per variant.** Below this, statistical significance is unreliable.
- **Wait 24 hours before calling a winner.** Open patterns vary by time zone and device. Early results skew toward mobile openers.
- **Three-variant framework per email:**
  - Variant A (Safe Bet): proven formula, optimized for open rate
  - Variant B (Bold Play): higher risk, pattern interrupt or emotion, may polarize
  - Variant C (Personal Touch): feels like a message from a friend, optimized for trust and reply rate
- **Each variant should use a different formula category.** Do not generate three curiosity gaps.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- **Clickbait without payoff:** Subject promises something the email does not deliver. Trains subscribers to ignore you.
- **Fake RE:/FWD:** Deceptive threading. Damages trust immediately and may violate anti-spam laws.
- **ALL CAPS:** Spam trigger. Reads as desperation.
- **Excessive punctuation:** "HUGE SALE!!!" signals low-quality sender.
- **Generic formats:** "Newsletter #47" or "[COMPANY] Weekly Update" — gives no reason to open.
- **Same style every time:** Predictability kills curiosity. Rotate between formulas.
