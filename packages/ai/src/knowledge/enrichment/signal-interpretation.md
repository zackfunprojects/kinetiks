# Signal Interpretation

How to read and act on prospect signals. Hiring patterns, funding events, tech stack changes, executive moves, and content engagement as buying indicators.

---

## Signal Categories

### Hiring Signals (Strongest)

**What to look for:**
- Job posting for role your product replaces or augments
- Job posting that mentions your product category or competitor
- Rapid hiring in the department your product serves
- Executive search in your buyer's function

**How to interpret:**
- New role = budget allocated, problem acknowledged
- Mentions competitor = actively evaluating, you have a window
- Rapid hiring = scaling pain, need for efficiency tools
- Executive search = change agent incoming, open to new tools

**Timing:** Act within 48h of posting. After 2 weeks the role may be filled or deprioritized.

**Personalization hook:** "Noticed you're hiring a [role]. That usually means [problem] is becoming a priority. We help teams like yours [solve that problem] without adding headcount."

### Funding Signals

**What to look for:**
- Seed/Series A: building the team, buying first tools
- Series B+: scaling operations, replacing manual processes
- Undisclosed round: often means they're being quiet about growth plans

**How to interpret:**
- Post-funding = buying window (30-90 days of tool evaluation)
- The amount tells you budget capacity
- The investors tell you strategic direction

**Timing:** Best window is 2-6 weeks post-announcement. Too early and they're still hiring. Too late and tools are already selected.

### Tech Stack Signals

**What to look for:**
- Adopted a complementary tool (your product integrates well)
- Dropped a competitor (they're looking for alternatives)
- Added infrastructure that your product sits on top of

**How to interpret:**
- Complementary adoption = they're investing in the category
- Competitor churn = active pain, urgent need for replacement
- Infrastructure change = modernization, open to new stack

### Executive Move Signals

**What to look for:**
- New VP/Director in your buyer's function
- Promotion of an internal champion
- Departure of a blocker or decision-maker

**How to interpret:**
- New executives bring their toolkits. First 90 days = highest receptivity
- Internal promotion = continuity but fresh mandate to improve
- Departure of blocker = remove objections that stalled previous attempts

### Content Engagement Signals

**What to look for:**
- Downloaded your whitepaper or guide
- Attended your webinar
- Commented on or shared your social content
- Visited your pricing page (if tracked)

**How to interpret:**
- Content download = researching the problem space
- Webinar attendance = investing time, likely evaluating
- Social engagement = aware of you, open to conversation
- Pricing page = actively evaluating, high intent

**Timing:** Respond within 24h. Engagement attention is perishable.

## Signal Decay

Signals lose value over time:
- **0-7 days:** fresh, high value, act immediately
- **7-30 days:** warm, still relevant, reference the signal
- **30-90 days:** cooling, may need a new angle or updated signal
- **90+ days:** stale, treat as new outreach, find a fresh signal

## Signal Stacking

Multiple signals from the same account compound:
- 1 signal = interesting, worth a look
- 2 signals = pattern forming, prioritize
- 3+ signals = active buying behavior, escalate to immediate outreach

When stacking, lead with the strongest/most recent signal and reference others as supporting context.
