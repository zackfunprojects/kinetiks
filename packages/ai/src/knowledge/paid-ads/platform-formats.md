# Platform-Specific Ad Formats and Constraints

Reference for creating paid ad creative that respects each platform's technical constraints and user behavior patterns. Every platform has different rules -- violate them and your ad underperforms regardless of how good the copy is.

---

## Google Ads (Search)

Text-only environment. Every character counts.

**Character limits:**
- Headlines: 3 slots x 30 characters each
- Descriptions: 2 slots x 90 characters each

**Headline strategy:**
- Headline 1 = keyword match. Mirror the search query as closely as possible. This is what earns the click.
- Headline 2 = value proposition. The primary benefit or differentiator.
- Headline 3 = CTA or social proof. "Start Free Trial" or "Trusted by 5,000+ Teams."

**Description strategy:**
- Expand on the value proposition from Headline 2
- Address the top objection for this keyword (price, complexity, switching cost)
- End with a clear CTA and what happens next ("See pricing instantly" > "Learn more")

**Responsive Search Ads (RSAs):**
- Provide 10+ headline variations and 4+ description variations
- Google tests combinations automatically -- give it diverse material
- Pin Headline 1 (keyword match) but leave other positions flexible
- Include at least 2 headlines with numbers, 2 with benefits, 2 with CTAs

## Google Ads (Display and YouTube)

Visual-first environments. The image or video does the heavy lifting.

**Display ads:**
- Responsive display ads adapt to available placements -- provide multiple asset sizes
- Keep text on images minimal (headline + one short phrase). The ad unit already shows text fields.
- Headline: 30 chars. Long headline: 90 chars. Description: 90 chars.
- High-contrast visuals. The ad competes with page content for attention.

**YouTube ads:**
- Skippable in-stream: hook in the first 5 seconds. If they skip, you pay nothing -- but the hook must earn the watch.
- Sweet spot: 15-30 seconds for awareness, 60-90 seconds for consideration
- First 5 seconds: state the problem or make a bold claim. No logos, no intros.
- End with a clear CTA + display URL. Verbal CTA + on-screen CTA together.
- Bumper ads (6 seconds, non-skippable): one message only. Problem or brand recall.

## LinkedIn Ads

Professional context. Higher CPCs, but unmatched B2B targeting precision.

**Single image ads:**
- Image: 1200x627 (landscape) or 600x600 (square, higher engagement on mobile)
- Intro text: up to 600 chars, but only ~150 visible before "see more." Front-load the hook.
- Headline: 70 chars. This appears below the image.
- Keep intro text conversational. LinkedIn users scroll a feed -- write like a post, not an ad.

**Carousel ads:**
- 2-10 cards, each with its own image and headline
- Use for storytelling: problem on card 1 → insight on card 2 → solution on card 3 → proof on card 4 → CTA on card 5
- Alternative: feature showcase. One feature per card with a benefit-focused headline.
- Each card should work standalone (users may not see all cards)

**Message ads (Sponsored InMail):**
- Subject line: 60 chars. Must feel personal, not promotional.
- Body: up to 1500 chars. Write in first person. Conversational tone.
- One CTA button only. Multiple CTAs kill conversion rates.
- Best for: event invitations, demo requests, high-value offers

**Targeting (LinkedIn's superpower):**
- Job title, job function, seniority level
- Company size, company name, industry
- Skills, groups, education
- Layer targeting dimensions: "VP of Marketing" + "SaaS" + "51-200 employees" = precise ICP

## Meta (Facebook and Instagram)

Algorithm-driven distribution. Creative quality determines reach.

**Text limits (recommended for full visibility):**
- Primary text: 125 chars visible without truncation (up to 2200 allowed)
- Headline: 40 chars (appears below image/video)
- Link description: 30 chars

**Image ads:**
- 1080x1080 (square, best for feed) or 1200x628 (landscape)
- Minimal text on image -- Meta's algorithm deprioritizes text-heavy images
- High-contrast, thumb-stopping visuals. Faces perform well.

**Video ads:**
- 15 seconds optimal for feed. Up to 60 for engaged audiences.
- Captions are mandatory -- 85% of feed video is watched without sound
- Square (1:1) or vertical (4:5) for feed. Vertical (9:16) for Stories and Reels.

**Carousel ads:**
- 2-10 cards. Each card: image + headline + link
- Story arc across cards: problem → agitation → solution → proof → CTA
- First card must earn the swipe. Last card must carry the CTA.

**Advantage+ creative:**
- Provide multiple text variants, image variants, and headline variants
- Meta's algorithm tests combinations and optimizes for conversions
- Give it diverse material -- different angles, different proof points, different CTAs

## TikTok Ads

Native-first platform. Ads that look like ads fail.

**Format:**
- 9:16 vertical video, always
- 15-60 seconds. Under 30 is the sweet spot for most objectives.
- Must look and feel like organic TikTok content, not a produced commercial

**Creative rules:**
- Hook in the first 2 seconds. Open with motion, a bold statement, or a pattern interrupt.
- Use trending sounds and formats when relevant. The algorithm rewards platform fluency.
- Text overlay for the key message -- many users watch with sound off despite TikTok's audio culture
- Fast cuts, movement, and direct-to-camera work best
- Lo-fi outperforms hi-fi. Phone-shot > studio-shot.

**Spark Ads:**
- Boost existing organic posts as paid ads
- Higher engagement rates than standard in-feed ads because they carry existing social proof
- Use Spark Ads for content that already has organic traction -- the algorithm compounds momentum

## Cross-Platform Rules

These apply everywhere regardless of platform.

**One CTA per ad.** Two CTAs split attention and reduce conversion. Choose the single most important action.

**Visual must work without text.** Many users skim past text. The image or video thumbnail alone should communicate the core message or emotion.

**Mobile-first design.** 80%+ of ad impressions are on mobile. Design for small screens, fat thumbs, and short attention spans. Test on a phone before approving.

**Message match.** The landing page must deliver on the ad's promise. If the ad says "Cut onboarding time by 60%," the landing page headline must reference onboarding time reduction. Mismatched messages cause high bounce rates and waste spend. Platforms reward message match with lower CPCs and higher quality scores.

**Platform-native tone.** An ad that works on LinkedIn will fail on TikTok and vice versa. Adapt the message to how people communicate on each platform. Professional on LinkedIn, casual on Meta, raw on TikTok.
