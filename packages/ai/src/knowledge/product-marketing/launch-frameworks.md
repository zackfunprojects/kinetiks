# Launch Frameworks

Structured approaches for feature announcements, product launches, and beta programs.

---

## Feature Announcement Framework

Every feature release needs four coordinated outputs:

### Changelog Entry
- What changed (specific, technical where appropriate)
- Why it matters (the user problem it solves)
- How to use it (link to docs or inline steps)
- Keep it scannable -- bullet points, not paragraphs

### In-App Notification
- Contextual reveal: show it when the user is in the relevant workflow, not on login
- One sentence: what it does + one CTA to try it
- Dismissible, non-blocking
- Never announce features the user does not have access to

### Announcement Email (3-Email Adoption Sequence)
1. **Awareness:** What shipped + why it matters + screenshot/GIF. CTA: "See it in action."
2. **Use case:** Mini story of how a specific team/person uses it. CTA: "Try the same workflow."
3. **Nudge:** For non-adopters only. Short, personal, direct. "Quick question: did you get a chance to try [feature]?" CTA: reply to this email.

### Social Post
- Per-platform format (see social/platform-playbook.md)
- LinkedIn: insight about the problem it solves + link in comments
- Twitter: bold claim about what it enables, thread if complex
- Visual required: screenshot, GIF, or short demo clip

## Product Launch Sequencing

### Pre-Launch (T-4 to T-2 weeks)

**Build anticipation:**
- Teaser content on social (the problem, not the solution)
- Waitlist or early access signup
- Beta program running (see below)
- Press outreach to niche journalists (not cold pitches to big outlets)

**Prepare assets:**
- Landing page copy finalized
- Launch email sequence written (5 emails)
- Social posts drafted per platform
- Blog post / announcement written
- Press kit assembled (one-pager, founder bio, screenshots, logos, metrics, testimonials)
- Support docs published
- Product Hunt listing prepared (if applicable)

**Operational readiness:**
- Support team briefed
- Sales team briefed
- Monitoring dashboards ready
- Rollback plan documented

### Launch Day

**Coordinated multi-channel execution:**
- Product Hunt post at 12:01am PT (if using PH)
- Maker comment within 5 minutes (the "why I built this" story -- personal, specific, vulnerable)
- Social posts staggered across the day (Twitter first, LinkedIn catches EU morning, email mid-morning)
- Respond to every comment within 1 hour
- Share progress updates throughout the day

**Product Hunt specifics:**
- Tagline: 60 chars max, outcome-first, no buzzwords
- Description: 260 chars -- problem, solution, proof
- Gallery: 5-8 images showing real product (not lorem ipsum), optional 30-second demo video
- Launch squad: 20-50 people who will engage genuinely (not fake upvotes)
- Stagger engagement across the day, not all at once

### Post-Launch (Week 1-4)

**Week 1:** Convert traffic. Capture emails aggressively. Send follow-up email to launch-day signups. Add social proof badges to site.

**Week 2:** Publish launch retrospective blog post with real numbers. Share learnings on social. Customer stories and testimonials.

**Week 3-4:** Long-tail SEO kicks in. Keep listings updated. Iterate based on feedback.

## Beta Program Design

### Three Phases

**Phase 1: Closed Alpha (10-25 users)**
- Hand-picked users matching ideal customer profile
- Daily or weekly 1:1 conversations
- Goal: validate core workflow, find showstoppers
- Duration: 2-4 weeks
- Exit criteria: 3+ users complete core workflow without help

**Phase 2: Private Beta (50-200 users)**
- Invite-only with application form to filter for fit
- Weekly email check-ins + in-app feedback widget
- Goal: validate retention, find friction, collect testimonials
- Duration: 4-8 weeks
- Exit criteria: 40%+ weekly retention, 5+ usable testimonials

**Phase 3: Open Beta / Early Access (200-1,000 users)**
- Waitlist with controlled access (50-100 invites per week)
- Automated onboarding with feedback triggers
- Goal: validate scalability, pricing sensitivity, support load
- Duration: 2-4 weeks
- Exit criteria: support manageable, pricing tested, onboarding works unassisted

### Beta Email Sequence

1. **You're In (Day 0):** Welcome + one specific first action + link to feedback channel
2. **Quick Win (Day 2):** Check if they completed first action. If yes: celebrate + next step. If no: re-explain value + simplify.
3. **Feedback Request (Day 7):** 3 specific questions (reply to email, no survey links): What would you change? Would you recommend this? What would make you pay?
4. **Graduation (Day 14-21):** Thank them, show what changed from their feedback, early access pricing or founding member offer.

### Feedback Collection

- In-app widget: persistent, non-intrusive, single text field, respond within 24 hours
- Weekly digest: compile into themes -- top 3 praise points (amplify in marketing), top 3 pain points (fix before GA), top 3 feature requests (roadmap signal)

## Launch Email Sequence (GA)

**Email 1: Launch Announcement (Day 0)**
- To: full list
- Content: problem, solution, proof, CTA
- CTA: "See it in action" or "Start your free trial"

**Email 2: Social Proof (Day 2)**
- To: full list minus Day 0 converters
- Content: 3-5 beta testimonials with specific outcomes
- CTA: "Join them"

**Email 3: Use Case Deep-Dive (Day 5)**
- To: openers who did not convert
- Content: mini case study, step-by-step workflow
- CTA: "Try the same workflow"

**Email 4: Objection Buster (Day 8)**
- To: non-converters who opened at least one email
- Content: address #1 objection with proof
- CTA: "See for yourself (14-day free trial)"

**Email 5: Last Call (Day 12)**
- To: engaged non-converters only
- Content: short, personal, direct ask -- "What's holding you back?"
- CTA: reply to this email (starts conversation)

## Press Coordination

**Media list:** 15-25 journalists covering your specific category, company stage, and audience's publications. For each: name, publication, beat, recent relevant articles, preferred contact method, what angle would interest them.

**Pitch:** 5 sentences maximum. What the product does (one sentence, no jargon). Why now. One proof point. The ask (demo, early access, interview). Why this journalist specifically.

**Embargo:** One publication for exclusive. Specific date/time. Complete assets provided. Follow up 48 hours before. Thank publicly after story runs.
