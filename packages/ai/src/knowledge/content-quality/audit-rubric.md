# Content Quality Audit Rubric

A systematic quality validation framework for self-checking generated content before surfacing it. Score every piece across all eight dimensions. No exceptions.

---

## 8-Dimension Scoring Rubric

Each dimension is scored 1 through 5. Apply scores honestly -- the rubric exists to catch problems before they reach the audience.

### 1. Voice Fidelity

Does the content match the loaded voice profile?

**Check:** Sentence rhythm, vocabulary choices, personality traits, banned words avoided, tone dimensions within range.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Generic AI voice. No trace of the target voice profile. Reads like any chatbot wrote it. |
| 2 | Occasional alignment. One or two phrases sound right but the overall feel is off. |
| 3 | Recognizable. The voice is present but inconsistent -- drifts in and out across sections. |
| 4 | Strong match. A reader familiar with the brand would accept this as on-brand with minor quibbles. |
| 5 | Indistinguishable. Sentence rhythm, vocabulary, and personality match the profile precisely. |

### 2. Hook Strength

Does the opening stop the scroll and create a reason to keep reading?

**Check:** First sentence and first paragraph. Does it contain a specific claim, a surprising fact, a direct challenge, or a genuine curiosity gap?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Opens with a generic statement. "In today's fast-paced world..." or "Content marketing is important." |
| 2 | Mildly relevant opening but no tension or surprise. Reader can predict the rest. |
| 3 | Decent hook with some specificity. Creates mild interest but does not compel. |
| 4 | Strong opening. Specific, relevant, creates a clear reason to read the next paragraph. |
| 5 | Scroll-stopper. Surprising, specific, challenges assumptions or reveals something the reader did not know. |

### 3. Structural Flow

Do sections build logically? Do transitions feel natural? Does each section earn its place?

**Check:** Read the H2s in sequence -- do they tell a coherent story? Read the last sentence of each section and the first sentence of the next -- does the transition work?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Random collection of loosely related sections. No logical progression. |
| 2 | Sections relate to the topic but the order is arbitrary. Could be rearranged without loss. |
| 3 | Logical order but transitions are mechanical or absent. Sections feel like separate pieces. |
| 4 | Clear progression. Each section builds on the previous. Transitions feel natural. |
| 5 | Inevitable flow. The reader could not imagine a different order. Each section makes the next one necessary. |

### 4. Proof Density

Are claims backed by evidence? Specific numbers, named sources, concrete examples?

**Check:** Highlight every claim. Does each one have a supporting proof point within two sentences? Count unsupported claims.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | All opinion. No data, no examples, no named sources. "Many companies find that..." |
| 2 | One or two proof points in the entire piece. Most claims are unsupported assertions. |
| 3 | Key claims have evidence. Some secondary claims float without support but the core argument is grounded. |
| 4 | Strong evidence throughout. Named sources, specific numbers, concrete examples for major points. |
| 5 | Every major claim has evidence. Numbers are specific (not rounded). Sources are named and verifiable. |

### 5. Audience Calibration

Is the depth, complexity, and assumed knowledge level right for the target audience?

**Check:** Identify the target audience's expertise level. Scan for unexplained jargon (too advanced) or over-explained basics (too simple).

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Completely wrong level. Expert-level jargon for a beginner audience, or kindergarten explanations for practitioners. |
| 2 | Mostly wrong. Occasional correct calibration but the overall level misses the audience. |
| 3 | Adequate. Mostly right level with a few sections that run too deep or too shallow. |
| 4 | Well-calibrated. Depth and vocabulary match the audience with minor adjustments needed. |
| 5 | Perfectly calibrated. Every explanation, example, and reference point matches what the audience knows and needs to learn. |

### 6. CTA Coherence

Does the piece lead naturally to an action? Is the CTA earned by the content that precedes it?

**Check:** Cover the CTA and read the piece. What action would you expect? If the actual CTA surprises you, it is not earned.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | CTA is disconnected from the content. Bolted on. "And that is why you should book a demo." after an educational article. |
| 2 | CTA relates to the topic but the content does not build toward it. Weak logical bridge. |
| 3 | CTA is relevant and the content loosely supports it. Acceptable but not compelling. |
| 4 | CTA feels like a natural next step. The content creates a need that the CTA addresses. |
| 5 | CTA is the inevitable conclusion. The reader arrives at it thinking "yes, obviously" before they see it. |

### 7. SEO Integration (Web Content Only)

Is the content structured for search discovery? Skip this dimension for email, social, and ad copy.

**Check:** Primary keyword placement, heading structure, internal link opportunities, search intent alignment.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No SEO awareness. Keyword absent from title and headings. No structure for search. |
| 2 | Keyword present but poorly placed. Headings are creative rather than searchable. |
| 3 | Primary keyword in title and H1. Some H2s are searchable. Basic SEO hygiene present. |
| 4 | Strong SEO integration. Keyword in title, H1, first 150 words. H2s answer related search queries. Internal links present. |
| 5 | Publication-ready. Keyword strategy covers primary and secondary terms. Headings map to search questions. Schema-ready structure. FAQ section addresses People Also Ask queries. |

### 8. AI-Tell Score

How many machine-generated patterns are detectable? Lower is better -- this is an inverse scale where 5 means fewest AI tells.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Immediately identifiable as AI-generated. Multiple tell-tale patterns per paragraph. |
| 2 | Clearly AI-generated to anyone who has read AI content. Patterns are frequent. |
| 3 | Passable. A casual reader would not notice. A trained reader would catch a few patterns. |
| 4 | Clean. Only one or two minor patterns in the entire piece. Requires close reading to detect. |
| 5 | Undetectable. No AI-tell patterns present. Reads as human-written throughout. |

---

## AI-Tell Detection Checklist

Scan the output for these specific patterns. Each one detected reduces the AI-Tell Score.

**Consecutive adverbs:** Three or more adverbs within a short passage. "Significantly," "remarkably," "incredibly," "fundamentally" -- especially when stacked.

**Hedge stacking:** Multiple hedges in one statement. "It is worth noting that it might be considered somewhat important to potentially consider..." One hedge is human. Three hedges in a row is machine-generated.

**Generic transitions:** "Furthermore," "Additionally," "Moreover," "In conclusion," "It is important to note that." These are filler. Replace with thought bridges that connect specific ideas.

**Uniform sentence length:** Four or more consecutive sentences within five words of each other in length. Human writing has rhythm -- long, short, medium, short. Uniform length is monotone.

**Empty intensifiers:** "Very," "really," "truly," "absolutely," "incredibly" used as amplifiers without adding meaning. "Very important" says the same as "important." Cut the intensifier or replace with a specific claim.

**Bolted-on warmth:** A paragraph of sentiment or encouragement that is tonally disconnected from the analytical content surrounding it. Common at the end of AI-generated pieces -- a sudden shift to "Remember, you've got this!" after pages of technical content.

**List-then-elaborate pattern:** Every section follows the identical structure: state a claim in one sentence, then explain it in the same way. Vary the pattern. Some sections should lead with evidence, some with questions, some with examples.

**Missing specificity:** Round numbers ("saves 50% of time"), unnamed sources ("studies show," "experts agree," "many companies"), and vague claims ("improves productivity"). Specificity is the single strongest signal of human writing.

---

## Minimum Thresholds

**Hard floor:** No dimension may score below 3. Any dimension at 1 or 2 triggers a mandatory revision for that dimension.

**Overall pass:** The average across all scored dimensions must be 3.5 or higher.

**Revision protocol when a dimension fails:**
1. Identify the specific failing dimension and its current score.
2. List the exact problems causing the low score (not vague descriptions -- point to specific sentences or sections).
3. Revise only the failing elements. Do not rewrite sections that are working.
4. Re-score the revised dimension. If still below 3, repeat.

**Priority order for revision:** If multiple dimensions fail, fix in this order: Voice Fidelity first (everything else is pointless if the voice is wrong), then Audience Calibration, then Hook Strength, then the rest.
