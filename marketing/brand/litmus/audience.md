# Litmus - Audience

## Primary Persona: "The Press-Hungry Founder"

### Demographics
- **Title**: Founder, CEO, co-founder at a growth-stage company
- **Company size**: 10-100 employees
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, fintech, healthtech, consumer tech
- **Experience**: 3-10 years as a founder, may have had 1-2 startups before
- **Age range**: 28-45
- **PR experience**: Minimal. May have tried one agency engagement that disappointed.

### Day-to-Day Reality
- Knows press coverage would accelerate growth but doesn't know how to get it
- Has tried emailing journalists directly with product announcements - got ignored
- Considered PR agencies but balked at $8-15K/month retainers
- Sees competitors getting coverage and wonders how they're doing it
- Has a great product story but can't translate it into a media angle
- Spends zero structured time on PR because there's no system for it

### Pain Points (Ranked)
1. **No PR expertise**: Doesn't know how to write a pitch, identify the right
   journalist, or time an outreach. The entire discipline is foreign.
2. **Agency cost**: Agencies want $8-15K/month minimum. At their stage, that's
   a full-time hire's salary for an opaque service.
3. **Journalist access**: Has no journalist relationships. Cold emails go to spam.
   Doesn't know who covers their space.
4. **Story identification**: Knows they have a story but can't articulate what
   makes it newsworthy to a reporter on deadline.
5. **Consistency**: PR requires sustained effort. Without a system, it falls
   off the priority list every week.

### Sophistication Level
- **PR knowledge**: Beginner. Understands that press is valuable but lacks
  tactical knowledge of how earned media actually works.
- **Marketing knowledge**: Moderate to high. Understands positioning, audience,
  and messaging from running the company.
- **Media literacy**: Reads industry publications. Follows reporters on Twitter/X.
  Understands media landscape from a consumer perspective, not a practitioner one.

### What They Value
- Credibility and third-party validation for their company
- Tangible outcomes: articles in named publications, not "impressions"
- Guidance and strategy, not just tools
- Cost-effectiveness relative to agency alternatives
- Speed to first result

### Where They Hang Out
- **Twitter/X**: Follow tech journalists and industry commentators
- **LinkedIn**: Post company updates, engage with industry peers
- **Communities**: YC alumni, Indie Hackers, founder Slack groups
- **Events**: TechCrunch Disrupt, industry-specific conferences
- **Podcasts**: How I Built This, The Twenty Minute VC, Acquired

## Secondary Persona: "The Marketing Lead Without PR Support"
- Head of Marketing or VP Marketing at a 50-300 person company
- Has budget for content, paid, and events - but no PR line item
- Occasionally gets asked by the CEO "why aren't we getting press?"
- More sophisticated in marketing but equally lost in PR mechanics
- Would use Litmus to build a lightweight PR function without hiring
- Values the campaign management and journalist tracking features
- Needs to show ROI to justify the spend internally
