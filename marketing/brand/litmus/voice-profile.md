# Litmus - Voice Profile

## Brand Voice Summary
Litmus speaks like a sharp, media-savvy communications director - someone
who understands how press works, respects journalists' time, and knows that
earned attention is worth more than paid. Professional but not stuffy.
Credibility-focused. Every word builds trust.

## Tone Attributes
- **Professional**: Media-grade communication. No sloppiness.
- **Media-savvy**: Understands the press landscape from the inside.
- **Credibility-focused**: Everything serves earned trust and authority.
- **Strategic**: Think before you pitch. Plan before you publish.
- **Measured**: Confidence without hype. Let the coverage speak.

## Pacing
- Clean, professional sentences. Medium length.
- Structured paragraphs that build an argument.
- Use headers and sections to organize complex PR strategy.
- Lead with the story angle, not the product feature.
- Mirror the cadence of a well-written press release - clear, credible, quotable.

## Vocabulary - Use These Words
- Earned, coverage, story, angle, pitch
- Journalist, reporter, editor, media, press
- Mention, article, placement, campaign, outreach
- Credibility, authority, trust, reputation, visibility
- Newsworthy, timely, relevant, compelling, exclusive

## Vocabulary - Avoid These Words
- Viral, buzz, trending (chasing virality is not PR strategy)
- Influencer (we work with journalists, not influencers)
- Spray and pray (we are the opposite - but don't use the phrase)
- Publicity stunt (undermines credibility)
- Impressions (vanity metric - say "coverage" or "placements")
- Content (too generic - say "story," "article," "pitch," or "coverage")

## On-Brand Examples
- "Earn attention. Don't buy it. Litmus builds the media relationships that turn your story into coverage."
- "A pitch that lands isn't luck. It's the right story, to the right journalist, at the right moment."
- "Your competitors are buying ads. You're earning front-page coverage. That's the difference."
- "Every journalist in your space has a beat, a preference, and a deadline. Litmus knows all three."

## Off-Brand Examples
- "Go viral with our PR automation tool!" (chasing virality, not our game)
- "Blast your press release to 10,000 journalists" (spray and pray approach)
- "Create buzz around your brand with AI-powered PR" (vague, hype-driven)
- "Get influencers talking about your product" (we focus on earned media, not influencer marketing)

## Writing Rules
1. Always say "earned media" or "earned coverage," never just "media coverage"
2. Journalists are people with names, beats, and preferences - never treat them as a list
3. Litmus is always capitalized when referring to the product
4. Pitches are "crafted," not "generated" or "created"
5. Use "coverage" and "placements" for results, not "mentions" (too passive)
6. When describing outcomes, lead with the publication name, not the metric
