# Hypothesis - Voice Profile

## Brand Voice Summary
Hypothesis speaks like a growth scientist - precise, curious, and allergic
to assumptions. The voice is experimental and data-driven, always questioning,
always measuring. We treat opinions as hypotheses and data as verdicts.

## Tone Attributes
- **Experimental**: Everything is a test until proven otherwise.
- **Data-driven**: Numbers talk. Opinions walk.
- **Precise**: Exact language. No approximations when data is available.
- **Curious**: Asking "what if?" is the engine of growth.
- **Methodical**: Step-by-step. Hypothesis, test, measure, iterate.

## Pacing
- Clean, structured sentences. Mirrors the scientific method.
- Lead with the question or hypothesis, follow with the test.
- Use data callouts and metrics prominently.
- Numbered steps for processes. Bullet points for results.
- Medium length. Not as punchy as Harvest, not as rich as Dark Madder.

## Vocabulary - Use These Words
- Test, measure, iterate, validate, optimize
- Hypothesis, experiment, variant, control, winner
- Data, conversion, lift, significance, confidence
- Page, template, element, section, component
- Launch, deploy, split, analyze, learn

## Vocabulary - Avoid These Words
- Believe, think, feel (we test, we don't believe)
- Beautiful, stunning, gorgeous (aesthetics without data are decoration)
- Best practices (untested assumptions disguised as wisdom)
- Guarantee, promise, ensure (we measure probabilities, not guarantees)
- Simple, easy (building good tests requires rigor)

## On-Brand Examples
- "You don't know which headline converts better. You think you do. Test it."
- "Best practices are just popular hypotheses that nobody bothered to validate."
- "Launch the page in 10 minutes. Get statistical significance in 48 hours. Ship the winner on day 3."
- "Every landing page is a hypothesis. Hypothesis helps you prove or kill it, fast."

## Off-Brand Examples
- "Create beautiful landing pages effortlessly!" (aesthetics without data, effortless claims)
- "Our best-in-class page builder guarantees higher conversions" (untestable claim)
- "We believe great design drives results" (belief over measurement)
- "Trust your gut and launch with confidence" (anti-data sentiment)

## Writing Rules
1. Always present claims with data or frame them as testable hypotheses
2. Hypothesis is always capitalized when referring to the product
3. Use "variant" not "version" when discussing A/B tests
4. Conversion rates use one decimal place: "3.2%" not "3%" or "3.24%"
5. Frame features as experiments enabled, not capabilities delivered
6. The word "test" should appear in most marketing content at least once
