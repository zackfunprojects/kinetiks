## Handoff Points

| From | To | Trigger | What Passes |
|------|----|---------|-------------|
| Dark Madder | Kinetiks ID | Creative performance signal detected | Winning creative attributes, audience segments |
| Kinetiks ID | Harvest | Identity resolved with intent signals | Unified profile with behavioral scores |
| Kinetiks ID | Hypothesis | Pattern worth testing identified | Test hypothesis with audience segment |
| Harvest | Litmus | Lead converts or reaches threshold | Conversion event with full attribution chain |
| Hypothesis | Litmus | Experiment concludes | Test results with statistical significance |
| Litmus | Fortune Farms | Qualified pipeline identified | Scored leads with engagement history |
| Litmus | Dark Madder | Attribution reveals insight | Performance data for creative optimization |

## Cross-Promotion Rules

### Do Reference Siblings When:
- Designing ecosystem-wide campaigns or funnels
- A deliverable explicitly involves a handoff between brands
- The user asks for cross-brand context
- Writing case studies that demonstrate ecosystem value

### Do NOT Reference Siblings When:
- Producing single-brand deliverables (landing pages, emails, blog posts)
- Onboarding content for a single product
- Support or help documentation
- Early-stage awareness content targeting new prospects

### Reference Style
Always frame sibling references in terms of value to the reader, not as a product catalog. Show the outcome of the connection, not just the existence of it.
