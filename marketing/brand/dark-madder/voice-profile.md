# Dark Madder - Voice Profile

## Brand Voice Summary
Dark Madder is named after one of history's oldest pigments - deep, rich,
unapologetic. The voice matches: edgy without being aggressive, confident
without being arrogant, dark without being nihilistic. We write like someone
who has seen too much bad content and decided to do something about it.

## Tone Attributes
- **Edgy**: We push against the bland. Corporate comfort zones are not welcome.
- **Proof-heavy**: Every claim has receipts. Data, examples, results.
- **Direct**: Short sentences. No warm-up. Get to the point.
- **Anti-corporate**: We reject buzzword marketing. Say what you mean.
- **Dark aesthetic**: Not grim - rich. Like the pigment we're named after.

## Pacing
- Short sentences dominate. Punchy.
- One-line paragraphs for emphasis.
- Occasional longer sentence to break rhythm and add depth.
- Fragment sentences are acceptable and encouraged.
- Stack three short lines, then one longer one. Repeat.

## Vocabulary - Use These Words
- Engine, learn, loop, evolve, sharpen
- Content, voice, signal, depth, craft
- Proof, data, performance, results, metrics
- Generate, research, plan, publish, iterate
- Dark, rich, layered, precise, intentional

## Vocabulary - Avoid These Words
- Content is king (dead phrase)
- Thought leadership (corporate cringe)
- Storytelling (overused to meaninglessness)
- Best-in-class, world-class (empty superlatives)
- Effortless, seamless (nothing worth doing is effortless)
- Curate (we generate, we don't curate)

## On-Brand Examples
- "Your content engine has a learning problem. It forgets everything after publish. Dark Madder doesn't."
- "Every piece trains the next one. That's not a feature. That's the architecture."
- "Generic AI content is a race to the bottom. We built an engine that races in the other direction."
- "Research. Plan. Generate. Publish. Learn. Repeat. Every cycle gets sharper."

## Off-Brand Examples
- "Create beautiful, engaging content effortlessly!" (too cheerful, too easy)
- "Our best-in-class content solution empowers teams" (corporate emptiness)
- "Tell your brand story with our storytelling platform" (buzzword soup)
- "Unlock the power of AI-driven content creation" (generic AI marketing)

## Writing Rules
1. Never use more than two exclamation marks in any single piece of content
2. Lead with the problem or the proof, never with the feature
3. Dark Madder is two words, always capitalized, never abbreviated to "DM"
4. The Learning Loop is a proper noun - always capitalize
5. When in doubt, cut the sentence in half
6. Numbers and data always beat adjectives
