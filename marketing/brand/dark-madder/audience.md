# Dark Madder - Audience

## Primary Persona: "The Frustrated Content Lead"

### Demographics
- **Title**: Head of Content, Content Marketing Manager, Senior Content Strategist
- **Company size**: 50-500 employees (scale-up with active content program)
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, fintech, healthtech, professional services
- **Experience**: 4-10 years in content marketing, often a team of 1-3
- **Age range**: 27-38
- **Reports to**: VP Marketing or CMO

### Day-to-Day Reality
- Responsible for 8-20 pieces of content per month across blog, social, email
- Has tried every AI writing tool and found them all disappointingly generic
- Spends hours editing AI output to match brand voice - sometimes longer than writing from scratch
- Publishes content with no feedback loop - no idea what style or angle performs best
- Under constant pressure to increase volume without increasing headcount

### Pain Points (Ranked)
1. **Generic AI output**: Every AI tool produces the same corporate-sounding content
2. **Volume vs quality**: Can't scale content without quality dropping off a cliff
3. **No learning system**: Each piece starts from zero. No institutional memory.
4. **Voice inconsistency**: Content sounds different depending on who wrote it or which tool generated it
5. **Research is manual**: Topic ideation, competitive analysis, and gap finding are still spreadsheet work

### Sophistication Level
- **Content expertise**: Deep. Understands content strategy, SEO, distribution, repurposing.
- **Technical**: Comfortable with CMS tools, analytics dashboards, basic automation.
- **AI literacy**: Power user of AI writing tools. Understands prompt engineering basics.
  Frustrated by the ceiling of current tools. Wants the tool to learn, not just generate.

### What They Value
- Quality that scales without hiring
- Tools that get better over time, not just faster
- Data-driven content decisions over gut feelings
- Authentic voice that doesn't sound like a chatbot
- Workflow efficiency - fewer tools, fewer steps

### Where They Hang Out
- **Communities**: Superpath, Content Marketing Institute, Exit Five
- **LinkedIn**: Active posters, engage with content marketing discourse
- **Newsletters**: Animalz, Every, The Hustle, Marketing Brew
- **Podcasts**: Everyone Hates Marketers, Content Briefly, Grow & Convert
- **Tools they already use**: Ahrefs, Clearscope, Surfer SEO, Google Analytics

## Secondary Persona: "The Agency-of-One"
- Freelance content strategist managing 3-5 clients simultaneously
- Needs to maintain distinct voices across multiple brands
- Values the Voice Engine for switching between client identities
- Time is money - every minute saved on editing compounds across clients
- Price-sensitive but ROI-focused
