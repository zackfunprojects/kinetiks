# Fortune Farms - Audience

## Primary Persona: "The Founder Who Does Everything"

### Demographics
- **Title**: Founder, CEO, solo sales rep, co-founder doing BD
- **Company size**: 1-30 employees (early stage)
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, agencies, consulting, professional services
- **Experience**: First or second startup. 2-8 years of hustle.
- **Age range**: 25-40
- **Reports to**: Themselves, their co-founder, or their investors

### Day-to-Day Reality
- Wears 5 hats. Sales is one of them, squeezed between product and operations.
- Has 1-3 hours per day for outbound. Maybe.
- Manages prospects in a spreadsheet or basic CRM they barely use
- Copies contact info from LinkedIn manually into email drafts
- Sends 10-20 cold emails per day. Reply rate is under 2%.
- Knows they need a system but every tool seems built for teams of 20+

### Pain Points (Ranked)
1. **Time scarcity**: Cannot spend hours on prospecting when they also
   run product, hiring, and fundraising. Need maximum output from
   minimum time investment.
2. **Tool complexity**: Enterprise sales tools require days of setup,
   training, and configuration. They need something that works in an hour.
3. **Poor targeting**: Sends emails to anyone who might be a fit.
   No systematic way to identify and prioritize the best prospects.
4. **No persona clarity**: Knows their product solves a problem but
   hasn't formalized who the ideal buyer is or what motivates them.
5. **Fragmented workflow**: LinkedIn tab, spreadsheet tab, email tab,
   CRM tab. Context-switching kills momentum and eats time.

### Sophistication Level
- **Sales knowledge**: Practical, not theoretical. Knows how to have a
  conversation and close, but hasn't studied sales methodology.
- **Technical**: Installs Chrome extensions without thinking. Uses basic
  SaaS tools daily. Not interested in APIs or integrations.
- **Outbound literacy**: Understands cold email basics. Has read a few
  threads on what makes a good subject line. Learns by doing.

### What They Value
- Speed to first result. Minutes, not days.
- Tools that require zero training.
- Concrete results: meetings booked, replies received.
- Low price point with clear ROI.
- Tools that don't try to be everything.

### Where They Hang Out
- **LinkedIn**: Both for networking and prospecting
- **Twitter/X**: Follow startup and sales advice accounts
- **Communities**: Indie Hackers, r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur, small founder groups
- **Podcasts**: My First Million, Starter Story, The SaaS Podcast
- **Content**: Short-form tactical content. Threads, not whitepapers.

## Secondary Persona: "The First Sales Hire"
- First SDR or sales rep at a 10-50 person startup
- Given a quota with minimal tools and no playbook
- Needs a system that comes with a built-in workflow
- More willing to learn tools deeply than the founder persona
- Values Greenhaus for persona building - helps them understand the buyer
- Will advocate for team upgrade once they prove results
- Price-sensitive but the company will pay for tools that generate pipeline
