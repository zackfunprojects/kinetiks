# Fortune Farms - Voice Profile

## Brand Voice Summary
Fortune Farms speaks like a no-nonsense farmer who happens to run a sales
operation. Direct, efficient, grounded in agricultural metaphors that make
complex B2B outreach feel intuitive. Plant seeds, tend the field, harvest
results. No corporate theater. Just work that produces.

## Tone Attributes
- **Direct**: Say it straight. No qualifiers, no hedging.
- **Efficient**: Every word earns its place. Waste nothing.
- **No-nonsense**: Skip the preamble. Get to what matters.
- **Grounded**: Agricultural metaphors keep things real and tangible.
- **Pragmatic**: Theory is for textbooks. We care about what works.

## Pacing
- Short sentences. Very short.
- One idea per line when possible.
- Bullet points over paragraphs.
- Numbers and specifics over descriptions.
- The rhythm of work: step, step, done.

## Vocabulary - Use These Words
- Plant, harvest, grow, cultivate, field
- Seed, greenhouse, bloom, root, yield
- Click, email, reply, meeting, close
- Prospect, target, persona, company, lead
- Results, pipeline, output, efficiency, return

## Vocabulary - Avoid These Words
- Ecosystem (too abstract for Fortune Farms' directness)
- Journey (there is no journey - there are steps)
- Empower, enable (too soft)
- Transform, revolutionize (too grand)
- Optimize (too corporate - say "improve" or "sharpen")
- Leverage (say "use")

## On-Brand Examples
- "One click. Two emails. Real results."
- "Bloomify finds them. Greenhaus profiles them. Plantr targets them. Harvest closes them."
- "Stop guessing who to email. Greenhaus builds the persona. Plantr finds the companies. You press send."
- "Your outbound isn't broken. It's unplanted. Let's fix that."

## Off-Brand Examples
- "Empower your sales team to transform their outbound journey" (corporate fluff)
- "Our innovative AI-driven platform optimizes your prospecting workflow" (jargon overload)
- "Leverage our ecosystem to revolutionize your pipeline" (every word is wrong)
- "Seamlessly connect with potential customers at scale" (says nothing specific)

## Writing Rules
1. Always capitalize product names: Bloomify, Harvest, Greenhaus, Plantr
2. Agricultural metaphors are encouraged but should feel natural, not forced
3. Use numerals for all numbers, even small ones: "1 click" not "one click"
4. Maximum sentence length: 15 words. Break longer thoughts into two sentences.
5. Lead with the result, not the process
6. When listing the tools, use the natural flow: Bloomify > Greenhaus > Plantr > Harvest
