# Harvest - Voice Profile

## Brand Voice Summary
Harvest speaks like a seasoned sales operator who respects your time.
Action-oriented, direct, and focused on outcomes. Every sentence should
feel like it's moving a deal forward. No fluff, no theory - just the
next step in the pipeline.

## Tone Attributes
- **Action-oriented**: Every sentence implies movement. Do, send, close.
- **Direct**: Say it in 10 words or fewer when possible.
- **Sales-native**: Speaks the language of pipelines, sequences, and conversions.
- **Confident**: We know outbound. We've done the reps.
- **Human**: Outbound that doesn't feel like outbound means warmth matters.

## Pacing
- Short, punchy sentences. Active voice always.
- Bullet points and numbered steps over paragraphs.
- Lead with the action, follow with the why.
- Use imperative mood: "Send the sequence" not "You can send the sequence."
- Rapid rhythm. Mirror the pace of a sales floor.

## Vocabulary - Use These Words
- Pipeline, sequence, prospect, convert, close
- Outbound, inbound, reply, open, click
- Contacts, deals, signals, timing, cadence
- Book, connect, engage, follow-up, qualify
- Bloomify, extension, enrich, capture

## Vocabulary - Avoid These Words
- Spam, blast, mass email (we are the opposite of this)
- Cold (we warm things up - say "outbound" instead)
- Automate (implies robotic - say "streamline" or "accelerate")
- Nurture (too soft for the sales context)
- Disrupt, innovate (empty startup speak)

## On-Brand Examples
- "Find the prospect. Build the sequence. Close the deal. Harvest handles the middle."
- "Your pipeline isn't empty. It's just not being worked. Let's fix that."
- "One Bloomify click captures the contact. Two emails later, you've got a meeting."
- "Outbound that doesn't feel like outbound. Because it starts with real signals, not guesswork."

## Off-Brand Examples
- "Nurture your leads through our innovative engagement platform" (too soft, too corporate)
- "Automate your cold email blasts at scale" (we are explicitly not this)
- "Our AI-powered sales solution disrupts traditional outbound" (buzzword overload)
- "Effortlessly manage your customer relationships" (too passive, too generic)

## Writing Rules
1. Always use active voice. "Harvest sends" not "emails are sent by Harvest."
2. Bloomify is always capitalized - it's the Chrome extension brand name.
3. Prefer verbs over nouns: "convert" over "conversion," "close" over "closure."
4. Numbers over adjectives: "3x more replies" beats "significantly more replies."
5. Never describe outbound as cold or spammy. We are the antidote to that.
